Dia-Control

Dia-Control is a social good platform by a medical institutions to empathize with those that have diabetes and also help them live a healthy life by checking their sugar level, food, insulin on the dia-control app.

The Overview

Until you are in peopleâ€™s shoe you may likely not know their pains and their conditions. People with diabetes are going through pains and some challenges that are not visible to people around.

We want to share the pains with them and that is why we are creating the app that will help them live a healthy life. Patients are having challenges with some Diabetes app out there and some of the challenges are

  • App not serving those that are visually impaired.
  • Cannot chat doctor for emergency.
  • Poor visual interface.
  • Lack of empathy.

Solution

Design a cross social good platform for people with diabetes to be able to measure or check their sugar level, insulin and also to maintain healthy diet, also add features that will help live a stress free life.

My Role

Conducting interviews, paper and digital wire-frame, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs.

Qualitative Research

  • I want to know what people think of a sugar level control applications.
  • Through research, I want to know what users want in the applications.

    • Interview Questions

    In the research phase, I interviewed eight people of different age in order to understand their challenges, pain points and goals. I told my respondents that there is no right or wrong answers and also got their permission to record the interview. Below are some of the questions

    Would you use a diabetes app?

    How often do you check your sugar level?

    What are your challenges with diabetes app?

    How do you feel when you forget to check your level?

    What feature would you like to see in a diabetes app?

    What interest you more in a health app?

    User Personas

    To classify the target audience and understand what motivates them, I created three personas This was done based on the survey results.

    Steve Jethro, Diabetes type 2

    Got ill with diabetes 6 years ago. I have to take injections.

    Sometimes I forget to take medication and go for activities that can help improve my health.

    I find it difficult to calculate my insulin. Most times I take too much of it.


    Goal:

    Quick communication, Develop reading habits.

    Mary Kyle, Diabetes type 1

    Got ill with diabetes 2 years ago. I have to take medications everyday.

    Sometimes forget to take medications and measure my blood glucose.

    Cannot control the amount of food intake and maintain a low carbohydrate diet.


    Goal:

    Quick communication, Develop reading habits.

    John Fred, Diabetes type 2

    Below are some of the high fidelity mockups

    Onboarding screen & Sign in and Sign up
    Dashboard and add up button screens.
    This is the screen for the Medication and Sugar Level. With the Medication option, user can add their medication and also set a reminder on when they would like to use their pills.

    The Sugar level was created with a nice interface for user to interact with. The sugar scanner can be added via bluetooth so that the sugar level can be added automatically once sugar level is checked.
    The user can connect their sugar level scanner via bluetooth. The goal is to make checking sugar level easier and stress free for users.

    Dark Mode

    Since this is a health app and we focusing on people with different abilities and disabilities so I created a dark mode option for those that are visually impaired.
    These are other screens like Reminder, Shop, Chat, Calendar, Payment page etc. I created the shop option for those who wish to buy their diabetes insulin pump or scanner. Users just need to place order and it get delivered.
